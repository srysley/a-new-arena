For over a decade, I lived inside the political arena. TLDR: I've entered a new arena.

Not metaphorically. Literally. Before I turned 26, I worked in congress, on campaigns, and was a candidate myself. My commitment to public service found its sharpest expression as a fundraiser for some of the highest-stakes campaigns of our lifetime, including Senator Jon Ossoff, Senator Raphael Warnock, and Senator Ed Markey. We raised $117M for Ossoff, and an average of $1M a day for Warnock during the runoffs. And on Markey's campaign, we made American history by outraising and defeating a Kennedy in Massachusetts. I learned that capital is belief, made tangible.

I have been slow to share this, but I walked out of the political arena in August 2025.

To the inspiring team at Arena, now part of Zinc Collective: mission accomplished. Watching two leading Democratic talent pipeline organizations merge together under one roof, with the combined resources to expand the talent pool and drive victories up and down the ballot, is exactly the kind of lasting infrastructure I wanted to help build. I'm proud of everyone and everything that made it possible.

While we were building the foundation for recruiting, training, and supporting political talent across the country, something had been quietly shifting in me.

Most people think the world they professionally operate in is "the" world. In reality, it's just "a" world. I spent 10+ years in a world of democratic politics, identifying systemic problems and leveraging my expertise in fundraising to support solutions. At some point, I began to understand a fundamental truth: money scales solutions in every industry. And I wanted to go deeper.

I couldn't get an MBA. Going to business school is simply a privilege I cannot afford. So, I had no choice but to do something different.

I'm a student of life and I've always believed the most powerful credential isn't your pedigree or resume, but knowing what you're genuinely great at, and betting on it. Working with Moshe Ovadia, a world class career coach, taught me how to identify and operate within my "zone of genius." So, I spent 15 months studying, learning, and preparing to enter a new industry. I thoughtfully activated my network for support. And, most importantly, I quieted a lot of noise and followed my intuition.

That intuition led me from public to private markets, specifically venture capital. The transition has been more straightforward than it sounds. A VC fund is a bridge connecting capital to ideas, not unlike a political action committee or campaign committee. Instead of donors, I work with limited partners. Instead of raising money for first-time candidates, I raise capital to finance early-stage founders. Same job function, different vernacular.

When I left Arena, I wondered if I'd ever find a team that matched the standard set by Lauren Baer, a former Congressional candidate and senior Obama administration official. She is a brilliant, strategic leader and I had the privilege of learning from her every day. Then Emily Wallach Leder introduced me to Ben Feder and Justin Yuan. It seems lightning can strike twice. The first decade of my career taught me that who you learn from is as impactful as where you work.

In January of 2026, I joined TIRTA Ventures as their Investor Relations Manager, working with Ben, former CEO of Take-Two, President of Tencent, and Board Member at Epic Games, and a team with a profound edge, expertise, and reputation in artificial intelligence and gaming.

The companies I'm now sitting alongside are rewriting how humans connect, learn, play, and show up at scale. That's what excites me.

My mission hasn't changed. The asset class has.

I still believe in building a more equitable world. I still believe that the right capital, flowing to innovative ideas and the builders behind them, creates a butterfly effect that outlasts any single campaign or news cycle. I still believe in paying it forward, loudly and often.
